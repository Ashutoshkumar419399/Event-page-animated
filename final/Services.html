WHAT ARE SOFT SKILLS?

Soft skills is a term used by employers to refer to the more intangible and non-technical abilities that are sought from candidates. It includes communication skills, presentation skills, self motivation, time management, personality development, creativity enhancing, etc.

It is the subtle behavior and communication style that help make a work environment or interaction with another person easier to manage. It also includes communication skills, such as voice inflection and gestures. Adaptability and flexibility are also considered as soft skills.

Why do soft skills matter?

You need hard skills to land a job, but you need soft skills to progress in your career. The reason a person gets a job, will keep a job and move ahead in that job has to do with people skills and people knowledge. In a similar vein, the Harvard Bureau of Vocational Guidance, in its survey of people who were fired from their jobs, found that for every one failure because of work skills, there were two failures in human relation skills. If functional knowledge were to be the sole criterion for success at work, ones success would be determined by the school and college grades. But real life proves to us every day that this logic doesn’t hold water. Organizations have turned their attention to soft skills during the selection process. They are encouraging their employees to enhance their soft skills through programs from in-house executives as well as external consultants. With the importance of soft skills gaining recognition, training to enhance an individual’s soft skills has risen in demand.

STORY TELLING?

Story telling is considered as an art. It is an imaginative process between the composer (narrator) and responder (listener) that invites us, as an audience to engage with the experience of others. The power of storytelling can create connections between people and allow individuals to find their inner self. Stories and storytelling are part of our day to day life. Humans have been telling stories since ages and now it has became the part of our DNA.

Data-driven information tends to speak to people's intellect – while stories stir their imagination and reach their feelings. It gives a heart to heart connection. That's what makes stories such a powerful tool to engage and motivate. Storytelling is powerful. As with the opening sentence it takes very little to immediately create imagery and emotion.

WHY STORY TELLING?

Storytelling is rightly becoming one of the most popular training programmes available.

While storytelling may not be the first topic that comes to mind when thinking of any professional work, understanding the importance of storytelling can give companies a competitive advantage, and individuals to improve themselves and learn something new. Companies want their customers to engage with them, both emotionally and intellectually and stories are one of the most effective ways of doing so.

It may seem counterintuitive to start with a story, but, because stories help us relate and are memorable, they can form business strategy and be a powerful tool in any stage of the innovation process. Storytelling can be a valuable tool to help understand the context or the challenges that a business or a company is facing.

Strategy can also be elevated with the use of story. This is because stories connect with people more than data. Strategies, and the stories attached to them, must be clear and concise.

What is corporate training?

It is a means of ensuring that employees improve skills and enhance performance by focusing on professional development. Employers utilize training to acclimate new employees, teach current employees new skills for the same job, and to advance employees into jobs requiring different skill sets.

Why is it important?

Corporate training has evolved rapidly in recent years to move beyond training just new hires, sales people and leaders. Learning and development professionals and corporate trainers are seen as strategic partners who are critical to the success of their business. Organizations want employees who are engaged with their mission, values and vision and who have the skills needed to improve performance. Updating employees’ skills and preparing them for career advancement not only provides a better trained and more productive work force. It also generates higher employee satisfaction and retention. Corporate training certifications allow employees to achieve recognition for validated proficiency and expertise in achieving business results. Your corporate training efforts will be successful with the right training development partner.
